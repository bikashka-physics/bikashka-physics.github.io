About

A short academic bio for visitors, collaborators, and conference contacts.

I am a researcher in theoretical and astroparticle neutrino physics, with interests spanning neutrino oscillations, quantum decoherence, open quantum systems, gravitational effects on propagation, and high-energy astrophysical sources. My work aims to connect microscopic physical mechanisms with phenomenological signatures in neutrino observatories.

Research

Current themes and long-term directions.

Neutrino decoherence

Open-system descriptions of neutrino propagation, Lindblad master equations, and phenomenological damping signatures in flavor-transition probabilities.

Gravity and lensing

Time-delay fluctuations, stochastic lensing substructure, and possible gravitationally induced decoherence effects for astrophysical neutrinos.

Astrophysical environments

Flavor evolution in magnetars, supernovae, neutron-star mergers, and high-energy source regions where matter, magnetic fields, and geometry influence neutrino signals.

Projects

Selected ongoing calculations and paper ideas.

Lensing-induced neutrino decoherence

Building a compact, dimensionally consistent decoherence parameter from stochastic gravitational time-delay fluctuations and comparing it with MDR-induced decoherence.

Modified dispersion relations

Deriving effective master equations for quantum-gravity-inspired dispersion corrections and mapping their energy and baseline dependence.

Neutrino magnetic moments

Studying magnetic-moment-driven helicity and flavor transitions in compact astrophysical sources and their high-energy neutrino signatures.

Flavor observables

Connecting source physics and propagation effects to measurable flavor ratios, Glashow resonance rates, and sky-dependent neutrino signatures.
